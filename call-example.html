mpp_define_domains( (/1,100/), 10, domain, & flags=GLOBAL_DATA_DOMAIN+CYCLIC_GLOBAL_DOMAIN, halo=2 ) defines 10 compute domains spanning the range [1,100] of the global domain. The compute domains are non-overlapping blocks of 10. All the data domains are global, and with a halo of 2 span the range [-1:102]. And since the global domain has been declared to be cyclic, domain(9)next => domain(0) and domain(0)prev => domain(9). A field is allocated on the data domain, and computations proceed on the compute domain. A call to mpp_update_domains would fill in the values in the halo region:

call mpp_get_data_domain( domain, isd, ied ) !returns -1 and 102 call mpp_get_compute_domain( domain, is, ie ) !returns (1,10) on PE 0 ... allocate( a(isd:ied) ) do i = is,ie a(i) = <perform computations> end do call mpp_update_domains( a, domain )

The call to mpp_update_domainsfills in the regions outside the compute domain. Since the global domain is cyclic, the values at i=(-1,0) are the same as at i=(99,100); and i=(101,102) are the same as i=(1,2).
